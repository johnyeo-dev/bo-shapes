What is B.O.?
When you see a weird or odd shape, don't panic! Break it into shapes you already know — rectangles, squares, triangles, semicircles, quadrants. Then add or subtract their areas to get the odd shape's area.

Odd Shape = Basic Shape A ＋ Basic Shape B － Basic Shape C …

Question 1 — Rectangle 80 cm × 60 cm with 2 Semicircles
Click each step → see it light up in the figure

Step-by-step working

1
Semicircle A (radius = 40 cm)
flat edge on bottom, dome curves up
= ½ × 3.14 × 40 × 40
= 2512 cm²
2
Semicircle B (radius = 30 cm)
flat edge on right side, dome curves left
= ½ × 3.14 × 30 × 30
= 1413 cm²
3
Triangle C — half the rectangle
(bottom-right half, diagonal top-right → bottom-left)
= ½ × 80 × 60
= 2400 cm²
4
Region D = A + B − C
(A and B overlap in the triangle region, so subtract C once)
= 2512 + 1413 − 2400
= 1525 cm²
5
Shaded = Rectangle − D
= (80 × 60) − 1525
= 4800 − 1525
= 3275 cm²
Shaded Area = 3275 cm²
Question 2 — Figure made of 8 squares. What fraction is shaded?
Click each step → see it light up

B.O. Approach — Basic shapes to find Odd shape

Key insight: The shaded shape is odd (irregular).
But the unshaded parts are triangles — basic shapes!
So: Shaded = Total − T1 − T2
1
Total figure = 8 unit squares
= 8 squares (given)
2
Triangle T1 — top-right unshaded
base = 2 squares, height = 1 square
= ½ × 2 × 1
= 1 square
3
Triangle T2 — bottom-right unshaded
base = 3 squares, height = 1 square
= ½ × 3 × 1
= 1½ squares
4
Triangle T3 — bottom-left unshaded
base = 2 squares, height = 1 square
= ½ × 2 × 1
= 1 square
5
Total unshaded = T1 + T2 + T3
= 1 + 1½ + 1 = 3½ squares
6
Shaded = Total − Unshaded
= 8 − 3½ = 4½ squares

Fraction = 4½ ÷ 8
= 9/2 ÷ 8 = 9/16
= 9/16 ✓
Fraction shaded = 9/16  → Answer (3)
