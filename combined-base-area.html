The Exam Question

Notice the widths! The tanks are drawn proportional to their base areas. Tank M is widest (1200 cm²), K is medium (900 cm²), N is narrowest (400 cm²).

Tank K holds: 50 × 18 × 40 = 36,000 cm³ of water. When we pour water into M and N, it must spread out — but how high will it go?

🤔 Think about it: A wider tank fills up more slowly (same volume → lower height). A narrower tank fills up fast. They'll balance at the same level — but what IS that level? We need a clever trick to figure it out!

💡 The Big Idea: When water reaches the same height in all connected tanks, it's exactly like having one big tank with all the floor areas combined!

All 36,000 cm³ of water sits across a total floor area of 2,500 cm².
Height = Volume ÷ Total Base Area = 36,000 ÷ 2,500 = 14.4 cm
✏️ Full Solution — Step by Step
Click "Reveal Next Step" to build the working one line at a time.
Vol. of water in K= 50 × 18 × 40
= 36,000 cm³
Base area of K= 50 × 18 = 900 cm²
Base area of M= 40 × 30 = 1,200 cm²
Base area of N= 20 × 20 = 400 cm²
Total base area= 900 + 1,200 + 400 = 2,500 cm²
Common height= 36,000 ÷ 2,500 = 14.4 cm
Vol. in K (end)= 900 × 14.4 = 12,960 cm³
= 12.96 litres ✓
🔑 The Rule: When water reaches the same level in all tanks,
imagine them merged into one Mega Tank. Then:
Common Height = Total Volume ÷ Total Base Area
